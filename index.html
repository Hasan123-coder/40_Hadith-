40 Hadiths from sahih Bukhari

1. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 8):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, Islam is built upon five pillars: bearing witness that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger, establishing prayer, paying zakat, fasting in Ramadan, and performing the pilgrimage to the House (Hajj) if you are able to do so.

2. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 5027): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The best among you are those who learn the Qur’an and teach it." This highlights the importance of learning and spreading the knowledge of the Qur'an as one of the best deeds a Muslim can perform.

3. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6064): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Do not envy one another, do not hate one another, do not turn away from one another, and do not undercut one another in trade. Be, O servants of Allah, brothers." This Hadith emphasizes the value of harmony, love, and brotherhood among Muslims.

4. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 2442): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Whoever relieves a Muslim of a burden, Allah will relieve him of a burden on the Day of Judgment." This teaches that helping others in their time of need is highly valued in Islam.

5. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 13): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"None of you will have faith until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself." This Hadith reminds Muslims to develop empathy and concern for the well-being of others.

6. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 5997): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Whoever does not show mercy to others, will not be shown mercy." This Hadith underscores the importance of mercy in Islam and the consequences of lacking it.

7. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 1): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Actions are judged by intentions, so each person will have what he intended." This Hadith emphasizes the importance of intentions in Islam. The reward or punishment depends on the sincerity behind one's actions.

8. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6114):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The strong person is not one who is strong in physical strength, but the strong person is one who controls himself when he is angry." This Hadith teaches that true strength lies in self-control and emotional discipline.

9. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6084): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Part of the perfection of one's Islam is that he leaves that which does not concern him." This highlights the importance of focusing on what is truly beneficial and leaving unnecessary matters.

10. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6017):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "If anyone asks you for something, give it to him if you are able." This Hadith emphasizes generosity and helps those in need.

11. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 1): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Verily, actions are judged by the intentions behind them, and each person will be rewarded according to what they intended." This teaches that the reward or punishment from Allah is based on a person’s true intentions.

12. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6237):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "A person is on the way of his friend, so let him examine whom he befriends." This Hadith advises Muslims to carefully choose their companions, as their behavior and character can influence one’s own actions.

13. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6987): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"Whosoever guides someone to goodness will have a reward similar to the one who performed it." This encourages Muslims to guide others toward righteousness and good deeds.

14. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 1419): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"The best of you are those who are the best to their wives." This emphasizes the importance of treating one's spouse with kindness, respect, and compassion.

15. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 2986):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The one who eats and drinks in the manner of the Prophet will be rewarded." This Hadith teaches the importance of following the Prophet's example in even the small details of daily life, including how we eat and drink.

16. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6416):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Be in this world as though you are a traveler." This teaches Muslims to not become overly attached to worldly possessions or desires but to remember the temporary nature of life.

17. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 16): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"The one who loves for Allah’s sake and hates for Allah’s sake is a true believer." This Hadith emphasizes that a Muslim’s love and dislike should be based on what pleases Allah, rather than personal feelings.

18. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 53):The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The most perfect of the believers in faith are those with the best character." This Hadith underscores that good character and ethics are among the most important aspects of a Muslim's faith.

19. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 220): The Prophet (ﷺ) said "Make things easy for the people and do not make them difficult, and give glad tidings and do not make them run away from the religion." This encourages Muslims to spread ease and positivity, rather than making the religion seem difficult or burdensome.

20. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 1521): The Prophet (ﷺ) said,"None of you should drink while standing." This Hadith provides guidance on proper etiquette, emphasizing good manners and avoiding undesirable behaviors in everyday life.

21. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 47): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "It is better for a man to be pricked by an iron needle than to touch a woman who is not lawful for him."

22. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6018): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Avoid the seven great destructive sins."

23. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 1373): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "He who does not thank the people is not thankful to Allah."

24. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 2796): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim."

25. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 707): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "If one of you intends to pray, he should cover his garments properly."

26. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 5938): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The most beloved of deeds to Allah are those that are most consistent, even if they are few."

27. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6125): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Allah has prescribed kindness in everything."

28. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6063): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Beware of suspicion, for suspicion is the worst of false tales."

29. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 2447): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "He who does not show mercy to our children, nor honor our elders, is not one of us."

30. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 706): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The believers, in their mutual kindness, compassion, and sympathy, are just like one body."

31. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 5703): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Facilitate things for people, and do not make it hard for them."

32. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 5683): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "There is no harm nor causing of harm in Islam."

33. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 76): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The strong believer is better and more beloved to Allah than the weak believer, while there is good in both."

34. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 2827): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "None of you will believe until you love for your brother what you love for yourself."

35. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 50): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The best amongst you is the one who learns the Qur'an and teaches it."

36. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 705): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Whenever a Muslim supplicates for his brother in his absence, the angels say: 'Ameen, and for you the same.'"

37. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 13): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The believer does not slander, curse, or speak indecently."

38. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 622): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The best charity is to satisfy a hungry stomach."

39. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 4): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Do not turn away a poor man, even if you have nothing to give but a half a date."

40. Hadith (Sahih Bukhari 6061): The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "He who does not thank Allah does not thank the people."
